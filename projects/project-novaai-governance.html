Frontier AI Governance Program — NovaAI Labs

Built an end-to-end AI governance program for a frontier foundation model provider: inventoried 8 material AI systems, classified each under the EU AI Act (2024/1689), and mapped the portfolio against the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) — identifying 2 GPAI with systemic risk, 1 High-Risk Annex III system, and portfolio-wide maturity gaps

Company: NovaAI Labs
Industry: Frontier AI / Foundation Models
Frameworks: EU AI Act (Reg. 2024/1689), NIST AI RMF 1.0, GDPR Art. 9
Scale: 2,500 Employees • 200M+ Consumer Users • 50,000+ Enterprise API Customers
Date: April 2026

1. Executive Summary

NovaAI Labs is a frontier AI foundation model provider headquartered in San Francisco with EU operations in Dublin. The organization's core product is Nova-7, a 175B-parameter large language model deployed across consumer and enterprise channels. With 200M+ consumer users and 50,000+ enterprise API customers, NovaAI operates at the intersection of the EU AI Act's most consequential obligations — Article 51–53 for General-Purpose AI with systemic risk, Annex III for high-risk use cases, and Article 25 for value chain responsibility.

This project delivers an integrated AI governance program combining three coordinated workstreams: (1) a complete AI system inventory of 8 material systems, (2) EU AI Act risk tier classification with obligation mapping and remediation priorities, and (3) a NIST AI RMF 1.0 maturity assessment across the GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE functions.

2. EU AI Act Classification Framework

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) establishes a risk-based regulatory framework. NovaAI's inventory was classified across all five tiers:

Risk Tier Regulatory Treatment
Unacceptable RiskProhibited under Article 5 — system must not be deployed
High RiskStrict pre-deployment obligations; conformity assessment required (Articles 6–15, Annex III)
GPAI with Systemic RiskModel evaluation, adversarial testing, incident reporting, cybersecurity (Articles 51–53)
Limited RiskTransparency obligations — users informed of AI interaction (Article 52)
Minimal RiskNo specific obligations under the Act; GDPR and sector rules still apply

4. NIST AI RMF 1.0 — Maturity Assessment

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework defines four primary functions. Each system was scored across all four on a 1–4 maturity scale (1 = Initial, 2 = Developing, 3 = Defined, 4 = Optimized).

Function Description
GOVERNEstablish policies, procedures, and oversight mechanisms for responsible AI development and deployment
MAPIdentify and analyze AI-specific risks including systemic risks, bias, safety, and security
MEASUREEstablish metrics, testing protocols, and monitoring mechanisms to track AI performance and risk indicators
MANAGEImplement controls, incident response procedures, and remediation capabilities for identified risks

Nova-7 Foundation Model — Overall Maturity: 1.75 CRITICAL

GOVERN2 (Developing) — CTO designated accountable; Board AI Safety Committee established March 2026 but operational effectiveness not yet demonstrated; AI Safety team experienced mass departures in 2025; no independent governance audit; GPAI-specific governance obligations not operationalized
MAP2 (Developing) — Internal red-teaming in place; systemic risk assessment not yet compliant with Article 53 standard; downstream use case mapping incomplete; no mechanism to track when Nova-7 is deployed in high-risk contexts
MEASURE2 (Developing) — Evaluations conducted internally but not independently audited; bias evaluation coverage limited for non-English languages (40% of usage); no societal risk measurement framework; no systematic hallucination or refusal effectiveness tracking
MANAGE1 (Initial) — No formalized AI incident classification; November 2025 DataPulse breach response was reactive; no model rollback procedure for safety-critical issues; post-deployment safety monitoring relies on user reports

Nova API Platform — Overall Maturity: 1.5 CRITICAL

GOVERN2 (Developing) — VP Enterprise accountable; AUP documented; no formal framework for Article 25 value chain responsibilities
MAP2 (Developing) — Use case patterns understood at high level (35% financial services, 25% healthcare); no systematic downstream high-risk assessment
MEASURE1 (Initial) — API performance tracked (latency, availability); no measurement of downstream harm
MANAGE1 (Initial) — API abuse detection in place; no management capability for downstream high-risk deployments; no incident notification framework to API customers

Critical Gaps: No systematic monitoring of downstream use cases; no deployer documentation; no downstream risk management; no incident notification to API customers.

NovaHire Recruitment AI — Overall Maturity: 1.0 CRITICAL

GOVERN1 (Initial) — Owner designated but lacks dedicated governance resources; no formal AI governance charter for high-risk recruitment AI; governance responsibilities dispersed; Board-level visibility extremely limited
MAP1 (Initial) — Bias audit identified 18% lower shortlisting for women in tech and 22% lower for candidates over 45; demographic breakdown incomplete
MEASURE1 (Initial) — Bias metrics identified but no continuous monitoring post-remediation; no defined accuracy benchmarks
MANAGE1 (Initial) — Remediation in progress; no procedure for new bias detection post-remediation; no AI incident classification

Critical Issues: No human-in-the-loop override, no Board reporting, no continuous fairness monitoring, no EU database registration, and no conformity assessment.

NovaChat Consumer — Overall Maturity: 2.0 HIGH

VP Consumer Products accountable; Trust & Safety team manages content safety and user reporting; third-party VoxTech Inc. supplies speech-to-text integration. The November 2025 DataPulse breach exposed vendor governance as insufficient. User satisfaction tracked (4.2/5.0); content moderation highly automated with human escalation; no structured measurement of harm from AI outputs; no systematic false information tracking; incident response for AI-specific issues not formalized.

NovaSafe Content Moderation — Overall Maturity: 3.0 MEDIUM

VP Trust & Safety accountable with dedicated moderation team of 180+ FTE globally; content policy documented in a 120-page policy. Precision and recall metrics tracked monthly (94% precision, 87% recall); multilingual performance gap (English 94% vs. Spanish 76% vs. Arabic 71%); human review coverage less than 0.1%. Automated moderation with human escalation; 48-hour appeal response time.

NovaGuard Security — Overall Maturity: 3.0 MEDIUM

CISO accountable; security operations and threat detection procedures documented; threat intelligence vendor contract established but lacks AI Act compliance clauses. Detection rates tracked (99.2% of known threat signatures); false positive rate monitored (4.7%); automated threat response with human review for permanent actions.

NovaInsight Analytics — Overall Maturity: 3.0 LOW

CFO accountable through existing finance governance framework; internal use only with no downstream deployment; forecast accuracy tracked (MAPE 12% for revenue forecasts); Finance team reviews all outputs prior to decision-making. Annual governance review sufficient.

NovaVoice Pilot — Overall Maturity: 1.0 HIGH (Pre-Production)

Head of Emerging Products accountable for pilot (5,000 beta users); no formal governance sign-off on production transition criteria; emotion recognition feature integrated; VoxTech Inc. provides speech recognition engine but vendor governance not assessed. Biometric voiceprint risk identified; speech recognition accuracy tested on limited population (92.3% WER on English); no bias assessment across accents/languages; no privacy impact assessment.

5. Portfolio-Level Risk Concentration

Two of NovaAI's eight AI systems carry GPAI with systemic risk obligations under EU AI Act Article 53: Nova-7 Foundation Model and Nova API Platform. These systems enable downstream high-risk deployments by enterprise customers with limited NovaAI oversight. One system (NovaHire) is classified as high-risk with documented discrimination risks and no completed conformity assessment.

The AI Safety team instability (mass departures in 2025) creates a cross-cutting governance vulnerability. The Board AI Safety Committee is too nascent to provide effective oversight.

NovaAI's most critical gap is limited visibility into downstream high-risk deployments by API customers. This represents a value chain responsibility issue under EU AI Act Article 25, affecting the entire Nova API platform and creating potential liability for NovaAI if customers deploy the model in high-risk contexts without adequate safeguards.

6. Priority Remediation Roadmap

Immediate Actions (Next 30 Days)

  • Stabilize AI Safety team: conduct external evaluation; develop retention strategy
  • Nova-7 and Nova API Platform: complete systemic risk assessments compliant with EU AI Act Article 53
  • NovaHire: implement human-in-the-loop override requirement for ALL shortlisting decisions
  • NovaHire: establish Board-level Fairness Oversight Committee with quarterly reporting
  • All systems: conduct preliminary gap analysis against NIST AI RMF

Medium-Term Actions (Next 90 Days)

  • Nova-7: establish formal AI incident response procedure; commission independent model evaluation audit
  • Nova API: implement downstream high-risk use case detection system
  • Nova API: create deployer documentation package for enterprise customers (Article 13 information)
  • NovaHire: complete bias remediation and commission independent fairness audit
  • NovaHire: establish continuous fairness monitoring dashboard; register in EU high-risk AI database
  • NovaVoice: complete governance gate criteria for production transition; DPIA; update VoxTech contract
  • NovaSafe: address multilingual over-blocking bias; increase human review sampling to 1–2% of decisions
