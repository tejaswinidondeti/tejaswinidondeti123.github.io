Third-Party Risk Management Assessment — FinSecure Banking

Vendor due diligence assessment of a payment processor for a 1,200-employee FDIC-insured bank; evaluated 4 vendor risks, verified 6 security controls, and identified 3 remediation issues under OCC and PCI DSS requirements

Institution: FinSecure Banking
Industry: Banking / Financial Services
Vendor: SwiftPay Solutions (Payment Processor)
Frameworks: PCI DSS v4.0, SOC 2 Type II, OCC Bulletin 2013-29
Date: March 2026

1. Executive Summary

FinSecure Banking, a mid-size FDIC-insured financial institution with 1,200 employees, is integrating SwiftPay Solutions as a third-party payment processor. SwiftPay will have direct access to FinSecure's cardholder data environment (CDE), processing credit and debit card transactions for the bank's retail and commercial customers.

This assessment covers a complete vendor profile, 4 vendor-specific risks with likelihood and impact scoring, verification of 6 security controls mapped to PCI DSS and SOC 2, and 3 remediation issues identified that require resolution before or shortly after full production integration.

VR-01: Cardholder Data Breach at SwiftPay CRITICAL — 15

  • SwiftPay processes 2.3M card transactions/month with access to PANs, expiration dates, and CVVs during authorization
  • A breach would require: IR activation, card reissue ($5–$25/card), OCC notification, Visa/Mastercard fines, and customer notification
  • Under card network rules, acquiring bank (FinSecure) is financially responsible even if breach occurs at third-party processor

VR-02: Payment Processing Service Outage HIGH — 12

  • SwiftPay SLA: 99.95% uptime (~4.38 hours/year allowed), RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 15 minutes
  • Outage would block all credit/debit card transactions at merchants and ATMs for FinSecure customers
  • OCC considers payment processing availability critical for safe and reliable banking operations

VR-03: Vendor Regulatory Non-Compliance HIGH — 10

  • If SwiftPay loses PCI DSS certification or receives negative SOC 2 opinion, FinSecure could be non-compliant with OCC third-party requirements
  • Consequences: OCC enforcement actions, MRAs in examination report, CAMELS rating impact, possible card processing restrictions

VR-04: Fourth-Party Subcontractor Risk HIGH — 12

  • SwiftPay uses Stripe (backup processor) and AWS (infrastructure) — FinSecure has no direct contractual relationship
  • SOC 2 report uses carve-out method for Stripe — Stripe's controls excluded from auditor testing
  • No notification mechanism when SwiftPay onboards new sub-service organizations

Control 1: Cardholder Data Encryption Partially Effective

  • AES-256 encryption at rest via AWS KMS with annual key rotation; TLS 1.2 minimum for all API connections
  • PCI DSS AOC confirms compliance with Requirements 3 and 4; SOC 2 audit confirmed encryption controls effective
  • Gap: Has not upgraded to TLS 1.3 (current industry best practice per NIST SP 800-52 Rev.2 and PCI DSS v4.0 guidance)

Control 2: Access Control to CDE Effective

  • RBAC with only 23 of 450 employees authorized to access CDE, each with role-specific limited permissions
  • MFA required: hardware tokens for admins, TOTP for standard users; quarterly access reviews with internal audit
  • All CDE access logged and monitored via SIEM; terminated employee access revoked within 4 hours
  • SOC 2 auditors found no exceptions in access provisioning, modification, or revocation

Control 3: Vulnerability Management & Patching Effective

  • Monthly internal scans (Qualys), quarterly external ASV scans (Trustwave)
  • Critical CVEs (CVSS 9.0+) patched within 72 hours; High (7.0–8.9) within 14 days
  • Annual pen test by NCC Group (Oct 2025): 2 medium findings, 0 critical/high, both remediated within 30 days
  • SOC 2 audit confirmed vulnerability management operating effectively

Control 4: Incident Response & Notification Partially Effective

  • Documented IR plan with severity levels, escalation procedures, and annual tabletop exercises
  • Gap: SwiftPay's 72-hour client notification SLA does not meet FinSecure's 24-hour requirement
  • SOC 2 auditor noted "other matter" observation — one notification sent at 71 hours (near limit)
  • FDIC requires banks to report security incidents within 36 hours, which is impossible if vendor waits 72 hours

Control 5: Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery Effective

  • Primary: AWS us-east-1 (Virginia); DR: us-west-2 (Oregon); RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 15 minutes
  • RDS Multi-AZ for database failover; S3 cross-region replication for static data
  • Latest DR test (Aug 2025): recovered in 3.2 hours with 8 minutes data loss — exceeded targets
  • BCP reviewed quarterly and approved by executive leadership; SOC 2 audit confirmed

Control 6: Logging, Monitoring & Audit Trail Effective

  • 24/7 SOC monitoring all CDE systems; every access logged (user ID, timestamp, action, success/failure)
  • Splunk SIEM with automated alerts: failed logins, off-hours access, large data queries, privilege escalation
  • Logs retained 12 months online + 7 years cold storage (meets PCI DSS Requirement 10)
  • SOC 2 audit confirmed monitoring and alert investigation within required timeframes

5. Issue Tracker — 3 Remediation Issues

Issue 1: Incident Notification SLA Gap HIGH

CategoryProcess Gap
OwnerTPRM Lead + SwiftPay Account Manager
Due DateJune 30, 2026 (90 days from contract execution)
  • SwiftPay's 72-hour notification SLA exceeds FinSecure's 24-hour requirement and FDIC's 36-hour deadline
  • Remediation: Amend contract to reduce notification to 24 hours for incidents involving cardholder data, PII, or outages >30 minutes; require quarterly incident reports

Issue 2: TLS 1.2 Instead of TLS 1.3 MEDIUM

CategoryTechnical Debt
OwnerSwiftPay VP of Engineering + FinSecure ISO
Due DateDecember 31, 2026 (within 12 months)
  • TLS 1.2 meets current PCI DSS standards but TLS 1.3 is recommended by NIST SP 800-52 Rev.2 and PCI DSS v4.0
  • Remediation: SwiftPay must provide documented migration plan with milestones; completion by Dec 2026 as contractual obligation (non-blocking for initial integration)

Issue 3: Limited Fourth-Party Visibility HIGH

CategoryCompliance Issue
OwnerTPRM Lead + SwiftPay Chief Compliance Officer
Due DateSeptember 30, 2026 (180 days from contract execution)
  • SOC 2 carve-out method excludes Stripe's controls from audit; no notification of new sub-service organizations
  • Remediation: Obtain Stripe's SOC 2 report, require 30-day notification for new subcontractors handling cardholder data, maintain sub-service organization register in annual due diligence package
