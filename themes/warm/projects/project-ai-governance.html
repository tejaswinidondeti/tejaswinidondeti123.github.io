AI Governance & ISO 42001 Implementation — MindfulAI Labs

End-to-end AI Management System (AIMS) aligned to ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — 4 core AI policies, Model Cards for 3 production high-risk AI systems, a 6-risk register driving all Critical / High risks to Medium or below, and a 38-control Statement of Applicability at 97% Implemented

Company: MindfulAI Labs (200 employees)
Industry: Healthcare & Financial AI
Frameworks: ISO/IEC 42001:2023, NIST AI RMF 1.0, EU AI Act, GDPR, PIPEDA
Scale: 5 AI systems inventoried • 3 production • Canada + EU

Project Overview

MindfulAI Labs is a 200-employee AI company headquartered in Toronto, developing machine learning models for healthcare diagnostics (medical imaging analysis, clinical decision support) and financial risk scoring (credit decisioning, fraud detection, AML). The company serves clients across Canada and the EU, placing it under the EU AI Act, Canada’s AIDA, GDPR, PIPEDA, and sector-specific regulations.

This project delivers an end-to-end AI Management System (AIMS) aligned with ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — the world’s first international standard for AI governance. The AIMS inventories high-risk AI systems, assesses AI-specific risks (algorithmic bias, model drift, explainability, data quality, adversarial robustness, regulatory misclassification), implements governance controls, and establishes policies for responsible AI across the model lifecycle.

Project Highlights

AI Systems Inventoried5 AI systems catalogued in the AIMS inventory — with full Model Cards for the 3 customer-facing production systems: MedVision AI (medical imaging CNN), CreditLens AI (credit risk scoring), and FraudShield AI (fraud detection)
Risks Assessed6 AI-specific risks on a 5×5 Likelihood–Impact matrix covering Fairness/Bias, Transparency/Explainability, Reliability/Performance, Privacy/Data Protection, Human Oversight, and Regulatory Compliance — 5 Critical + 1 High inherent, all reduced to Medium or Low residual
Statement of ApplicabilityFull ISO/IEC 42001 Annex A SoA across all 38 controls — A.2 Policies, A.3 Internal Organization, A.4 Resources, A.5 Impact Assessment, A.6 Lifecycle, A.7 Data, A.8 Stakeholder Information, A.9 Use, A.10 Third Party — 97% Implemented (37/38), 1 In Progress
AI Policies Authored4 core policies — Responsible AI Policy, AI Data Governance & Privacy Policy, AI Fairness & Bias Mitigation Policy, AI Incident Response & Escalation Policy — each linked to SoA controls and risk register entries
Model CardsStructured, auditable documentation for each production AI system covering intended use, data lineage, performance metrics, fairness evaluation, limitations, and human oversight — aligned to EU AI Act Articles 11 & 13, NIST AI RMF GOVERN/MAP/MEASURE, and ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 7.5
Frameworks AppliedISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AIMS), NIST AI RMF 1.0, EU AI Act (Reg. 2024/1689), Canada AIDA, GDPR Articles 22 & 35, PIPEDA
Regulatory ScopeHigh-Risk AI under EU AI Act Annex III, EU MDR Class IIa (medical devices), OSFI E-23 (Canadian banking), ECOA (US credit), Health Canada SaMD guidance
