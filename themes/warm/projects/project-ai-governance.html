Project Overview

MindfulAI Labs is a 200-employee AI company headquartered in Toronto, developing machine learning models for healthcare diagnostics (medical imaging analysis, clinical decision support) and financial risk scoring (credit decisioning, fraud detection, AML). The company serves clients across Canada and the EU, placing it under the EU AI Act, Canada’s AIDA, GDPR, PIPEDA, and sector-specific regulations.

This project delivers an end-to-end AI Management System (AIMS) aligned with ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — the world’s first international standard for AI governance. The AIMS inventories high-risk AI systems, assesses AI-specific risks (algorithmic bias, model drift, explainability, data quality, adversarial robustness, regulatory misclassification), implements governance controls, and establishes policies for responsible AI across the model lifecycle.

Project Highlights

AI Systems Inventoried5 high-risk AI systems — including MedVision AI (medical imaging CNN), CreditLens AI (credit scoring), and fraud detection models
Risks Assessed6 AI-specific risks scored on 5×5 Likelihood–Impact matrix using NIST AI RMF methodology
Governance Controls10 controls implemented across the AI lifecycle — data governance, model validation, bias testing, human oversight, monitoring, and incident response
Policies Established4 policies for responsible AI: AI Governance Policy, AI Risk Management Standard, AI Data Governance Standard, AI Human Oversight Standard
Frameworks AppliedISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AIMS), NIST AI RMF 1.0, EU AI Act (Reg. 2024/1689), Canada AIDA, GDPR Art. 22
Regulatory ScopeHigh-Risk AI under EU AI Act Annex III, EU MDR Class IIa (medical devices), OSFI E-23 (Canadian banking), ECOA (US credit), Health Canada SaMD
