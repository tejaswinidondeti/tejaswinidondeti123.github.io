NovaHire — AI Risk Assessment & Board Governance Review

Conducted a full AI risk assessment and Board-level governance review of NovaHire, a transformer-based recruitment and CV-screening system classified as High-Risk under EU AI Act Annex III — documenting 7 risks (4 Critical), confirmed bias outcomes across 180,000+ candidates, and a 90-day remediation programme escalated to the Board Risk & Audit Committee.

Company: NovaAI Labs
System: NVA-AI-003 • NovaHire v3.1
Frameworks: EU AI Act Annex III, GDPR Art. 22, NIST AI RMF 1.0
Scale: 180,000+ Candidates • 47 Enterprise Deployers
Date: April 2026

1. Executive Summary

NovaHire (NVA-AI-003) is an internally developed, transformer-based natural language processing system used for recruitment and CV screening. The system ingests CVs, job descriptions, and LinkedIn profiles to produce ranked shortlists with fit scores from 0–100. It is deployed within NovaAI Labs HR operations and licensed to 47 enterprise customers across the European Union, having processed 180,000+ candidates since October 2023.

This engagement combined a standalone AI Risk Assessment with a Board-level Governance Review Memo prepared for the Chief Executive Officer and Board Risk & Audit Committee. The review was triggered by a February 2026 independent bias audit that confirmed measurable discriminatory outcomes and a subsequent reassessment of NovaAI's obligations under the EU AI Act high-risk regime that came into force in January 2026.

Confirmed Bias Findings (Feb 2026 Audit): Women applying for technical roles were shortlisted at 18% lower rates than statistically equivalent men. Candidates over age 45 were shortlisted at 22% lower rates than statistically equivalent younger candidates for senior roles. These are measured outcomes across the full 180,000-candidate operational history — not estimates.

Material regulatory non-compliance was identified under EU AI Act Articles 10, 13, 14, and 43: no conformity assessment has been conducted; enterprise deployers have received no Article 13 instructions for use; and autonomous rejection mechanisms lack documented Article 14 human oversight across the 47 customer deployments. The Board was asked to approve immediate suspension of automated shortlisting within 72 hours, commence a 90-day remediation programme, notify affected customers, and engage external employment law counsel.

3. Risk Assessment Methodology

The assessment used a standard 5×5 likelihood-impact matrix. Risk scores are computed as the product of likelihood and impact to yield both inherent and residual risk ratings. Controls are proposed per risk and scored for residual exposure assuming successful implementation.

Likelihood Scale1 = Remote • 2 = Low • 3 = Medium • 4 = High • 5 = Almost Certain
Impact Scale1 = Negligible • 2 = Minor • 3 = Moderate • 4 = Severe • 5 = Catastrophic
Risk Levels1–6 Low • 7–14 Medium • 15–19 High • 20–25 Critical

6. Board Governance Review — Key Findings

The companion Governance Review Memo (Reference NVA-GOV-2026-003, CONFIDENTIAL — Board Only) escalated four material findings to the Board Risk & Audit Committee:

6.1 Classification and Regulatory Exposure

NovaHire is classified as high-risk under EU AI Act Annex III, Category 4(a). This triggers conformity assessment (Article 43), risk assessment and bias monitoring (Articles 6, 14, 16), deployment transparency and human oversight (Article 14), and deployer instructions for use (Article 13). NovaAI holds both provider and deployer status; the 47 enterprise customers inherit deployer obligations but lack awareness or supporting documentation to discharge them. The high-risk regime came into force in January 2026 — NovaAI is in material non-compliance.

6.2 The Confirmed Bias Problem — 18% and 22%

The February 2026 bias audit across the full 180,000-candidate historical dataset produced unambiguous findings: 18% lower shortlisting for women in technical roles and 22% lower shortlisting for candidates over 45 in senior roles. These are measured outcomes, not estimates. They mirror the age-discrimination pattern documented in the Workday class action (2024–2026). The outcomes cannot be defended — only acknowledged and remedied.

6.3 The Enterprise Customer Exposure Problem

  • No Article 13 instructions for use have been supplied to any of the 47 customers.
  • No customer attestations have been obtained for Article 14 human oversight.
  • No bias audit data has been shared with customers.
  • Current customer contracts do not include EU AI Act compliance clauses or liability allocation.

If a customer is sued by a candidate for discriminatory screening, NovaAI Labs will very likely be joined as a co-defendant provider. Liability exposure is direct and uninsured. Public disclosure of the bias audit is expected to trigger rapid enterprise customer churn.

6.4 The Autonomous Rejection Problem

Article 14 requires documented human review of all significant employment decisions. NovaAI implemented human-in-the-loop review for internal HR use in January 2026, but has no visibility into how customers configure the system. Some customers may be running fully autonomous rejection workflows. Technical enforcement via product controls — not merely contractual obligations — is required.

Residual Risk Position — Medium-High: Even with full remediation controls in place, the residual risk position of NovaHire remains Medium-High. Harm has already occurred; candidates have been rejected at discriminatory rates. Rectification and notification obligations may crystallise under EU AI Act Article 84. The 90-day remediation window is time-critical.

8. Board Decision Options

The Board was presented with three options. The AI Governance Office recommended Option A.

Option A — Immediate Suspension + 90-Day Remediation Recommended

  • Suspend automated shortlisting for all deployments (internal & customer-facing) within 72 hours.
  • Revert to full manual CV review during remediation period.
  • Execute all remediation actions on a 90-day timeline.
  • Maintain Board-level governance oversight of the remediation program.

Impact: Reduces regulatory and litigation risk. Demonstrates responsible governance. Operational disruption limited to 90 days. Revenue impact: ~$4.5M in additional operating costs.

Option B — Enhanced Oversight During Remediation Not Recommended

  • Retain NovaHire in production with mandatory human review of all shortlisting decisions.
  • Proceed with the 90-day remediation on the same timeline.
  • Assume human oversight controls are effective across 47 customer deployments.

Risk: Cannot confirm human-review effectiveness across the customer base. Continued exposure to discriminatory outcomes, regulatory findings, and litigation during the 90-day window. No technical safeguards. Reputational risk if the audit becomes public while the system remains active.

Option C — Full Product Withdrawal Disproportionate

  • Sunset NovaHire entirely; discontinue all deployments.
  • Revert to manual screening indefinitely.
  • Issue contract termination notices to all 47 enterprise customers.

Impact: Eliminates regulatory and litigation risk but produces very significant revenue impact (~$18M annual NovaHire licensing revenue). Disproportionate response if remediation programme succeeds.

10. Recommended Human Oversight Model

Immediate (Weeks 1–4)

Mandatory human review required for 100% of shortlisting decisions, across both NovaAI Labs internal hiring and all 47 enterprise deployments. System must technically enforce this requirement and prevent auto-rejection configurations.

Enhanced Monitoring (Weeks 5–12)

Quarterly demographic audits across all shortlists; tracking of human reviewer override rates; analysis of acceptance vs. rejection patterns by protected characteristic.

Governance & Accountability (Weeks 13+)

Establishment of an AI Governance Committee with quarterly review of all bias metrics; annual independent external audit; and formal escalation procedures for anomalous patterns.

11. Escalation Clause

If the 72-hour suspension of automated shortlisting is not implemented, the AI Governance Office will escalate this matter to the Chief Compliance Officer and will consider notification obligations to relevant Data Protection Authorities under Article 84 of the EU AI Act.
