2.2 Intended Purpose

NovaHire is a high-risk AI system designed to assist human recruiters in screening job applications. Specifically, it is intended to:

  • Parse CV/resume documents and extract structured candidate information
  • Compare candidate qualifications against job description requirements
  • Generate a ranked shortlist with fit scores (0–100) for each candidate
  • Highlight relevant experience and skills matching the job description
  • Flag potential interview discussion topics

What NovaHire Is NOT Intended To Do

  • Make final hiring or rejection decisions autonomously
  • Replace human judgment in recruitment
  • Predict future job performance with certainty
  • Assess personality, culture fit, or character
  • Evaluate candidates on any protected characteristic
  • Operate without human oversight for consequential decisions

2.3 Intended Users / Deployers

Enterprise HR teams and recruitment professionals; the NovaAI Labs internal HR function; and 47 enterprise customers as of April 2026 (list maintained separately). All operators must be trained recruitment professionals, must have completed NovaHire oversight training before operating the system, and must understand the system's limitations and non-deterministic nature.

2.6 Why NovaHire is Classified High-Risk

EU AI Act Annex III, Category 4(a) — AI systems intended for recruitment or selection. NovaHire directly influences employment outcomes for natural persons, has significant impact on individuals' access to employment opportunities, and carries potential for proxy discrimination via NLP features on CV content. Not a borderline case.

2.7 Known Limitations (Article 13 Disclosure)

  • Bias Risks: February 2026 audit identified 18% gender disparity in technical roles and 22% age disparity in senior positions in v3.0; v3.1 includes debiased training data and mandatory human-oversight enforcement; ongoing monitoring required.
  • Language: Performance best in English; may vary for other supported languages.
  • CV Format Dependency: Performance degrades on non-standard CV layouts.
  • Industry Dependency: Trained primarily on data from tech, finance, and professional services — performance may vary for trades, creative industries, or public sector.
  • Temporal Dependency: Resume screening reflects the state of the job market at training time; not suitable for roles outside standard practice.
  • Not a performance prediction tool — fit score measures alignment with the job description, not future job performance.
  • Not a personality or culture assessment — no behavioral or psychological predictions.

2.8 Prohibited Uses

  • Automated rejection without human review (technically enforced from v3.1)
  • Ranking candidates on any protected characteristic directly
  • Predicting future job performance, tenure, or loyalty
  • Making promotion or termination decisions about existing employees
  • Assessing candidates below the age of 16
  • Any use case outside professional recruitment
  • Integration into automated hiring pipelines that remove human judgment

2.9 Geographic Scope & Regulatory Basis

Approved: EU (Germany, Netherlands, France, Ireland, Spain, Italy, Poland, Sweden, Denmark, Belgium), UK, US (with state-specific compliance modules), Japan. Not Approved: China, Russia, sanctioned jurisdictions.

Anchored to: EU AI Act high-risk obligations, GDPR, EU Equal Treatment Directive, member-state implementing measures, US state laws (NYC Local Law 144, Illinois AIVIA, Colorado AI Act), and the UK Equality Act 2010.

3. Document 2 — Risk Management Summary

Doc ID: NVA-HRAI-DOC-02 • Classification: EU AI Act Article 9 — Risk Management System.

3.1 Risk Management Approach

A continuous, iterative process running throughout the NovaHire lifecycle, aligned to ISO/IEC 23894 (AI risk management) and integrated with the NovaAI Labs enterprise risk framework. Governance oversight sits with the AI Governance Committee; operational ownership with the Chief AI Officer and the NovaHire System Owner. Reviewed quarterly for high-risk systems and immediately upon any material change.

3.2 Risk Identification Methodology

  • Structured risk workshops with cross-functional stakeholders
  • Use-case specific risk taxonomy for recruitment AI
  • Pre-deployment bias evaluation
  • Ongoing monitoring of performance and fairness metrics
  • Incident-driven risk identification
  • External advisor consultation for known blind spots
  • Stakeholder engagement (deployer feedback, candidate complaints)

3.3 Risk Assessment Criteria

Risk Score = Likelihood (1–5) × Impact (1–5). Levels: Low 1–6   Medium 7–14   High 15–19   Critical 20–25.

Risk appetite: Recruitment AI should not carry high or critical residual risk.

3.5 Risk Treatment Strategy

Treatment options considered for each risk: Avoid / Reduce / Transfer / Accept.

  • Avoidance applied: None — NovaHire is continued in production with remediation.
  • Reduction applied: All 9 risks (primary strategy).
  • Transfer applied: Partial via cyber insurance for R09 liability.
  • Acceptance: None at inherent level; medium residual accepted under monitoring.

3.6 Lifecycle Integration & Fairness Approach

Risk management is embedded across the lifecycle: bias-aware architecture in research/design; fairness metrics computed at each training checkpoint; pre-deployment external red team and independent bias audit; continuous fairness metrics in production; risk reassessment for model updates; and data retention/deletion at decommissioning.

Fairness evaluation covers demographic parity, equalized odds (true-positive rates), counterfactual fairness, intersectional analysis (gender × age, etc.), annual external evaluation, and continuous in-production monitoring with alert thresholds.

Reporting: Material risks reported quarterly to the AI Governance Committee; critical risks reported immediately to CAIO, CRO, and the Board AI Safety Sub-Committee. Approved by the AI Governance Committee in April 2026.

4.5 Bias Identification & Mitigation

v1.0–v3.0 training data reflected historical hiring patterns that embedded gender, age, and potentially ethnic biases. The February 2026 audit confirmed an 18% gender disparity in technical role shortlisting and a 22% age disparity in senior role shortlisting; ethnicity proxy analysis is ongoing.

v3.1 remediation measures:

  • Rebalanced training dataset (equal representation across gender and age bands)
  • Temporal marker removal (graduation year, tech-stack vintage)
  • Name-inference ablation
  • Fairness-constrained training objective
  • Counterfactual fairness testing in validation

Ongoing monitoring: demographic parity, equalized odds, and calibration across groups.

5. Document 4 — Human Oversight Measures

Doc ID: NVA-HRAI-DOC-04 • Classification: EU AI Act Article 14.

5.1 Oversight Philosophy

NovaHire is a decision-support system, not an autonomous decision-maker. Three principles govern every oversight measure:

  • "Rubber-stamp" oversight is not oversight — human reviewers must have genuine capability to override.
  • Oversight must be meaningful: information, training, authority, time, and culture must all be in place.
  • Oversight is mandatory, not discretionary — technically enforced from v3.1.

5.2 Article 14 Objectives

Human oversight must enable deployers to: understand the capacities and limitations of NovaHire and monitor its operation; remain aware of automation bias; interpret NovaHire's output correctly; decide not to use or to override NovaHire output; and intervene or stop the system where safe to do so.

5.3 Four-Layer Oversight Architecture

Layer 1 — Operator Oversight (HR Coordinator / Recruiter)

Reviews the NovaHire shortlist before any candidate communication; must approve or modify it; must document reasoning for any override; no rejection letter can be sent without human approval. Technical enforcement from v3.1: the API call to "issue rejection" requires a human-approval token.

Layer 2 — Hiring Manager Oversight

Reviews shortlist for roles above a defined seniority threshold; provides second-pair-of-eyes on shortlisting decisions; can request additional candidates outside the shortlist.

Layer 3 — HR Leadership Oversight

Monthly review of NovaHire usage metrics; quarterly review of override rates and demographic patterns; escalation point for policy disputes.

Layer 4 — AI Governance Oversight (NovaAI Labs Provider)

Quarterly review of aggregated deployer usage data; monitoring of fairness metrics across deployments; incident response for concerning patterns; enterprise deployer audit rights.

5.5 Oversight Procedures (Daily → Quarterly)

  • Before go-live: deployer operations team trained; HR coordinators trained on override criteria; test scenarios completed; NovaAI Labs verifies training before API enablement.
  • Daily: HR coordinator reviews shortlist, approves/modifies/rejects, captures reasoning; rejected candidates communicated only after human approval; reviewer session logged.
  • Weekly: HR team lead reviews override patterns; anomalies escalated to AI Governance Office.
  • Monthly: HR leadership reviews dashboard; target override rate 10–25% — neither too low (rubber-stamp) nor too high (tool inadequacy).
  • Quarterly: NovaAI Labs and deployer joint review of demographic patterns and policy refinement.

Annual mandatory refresh; pass 80% knowledge check before API access.

5.7 Anti-Automation-Bias Measures

  • Interface design — scores shown alongside "qualification highlights" to encourage substantive review
  • Score interpretation guidance — fit score is not a performance prediction
  • Forced review delay — minimum time-on-screen before decisions can be finalized in batch reviews
  • Periodic devil's advocate prompts — UI suggests reviewer consider alternatives
  • Training emphasis — AI agreement is not a signal of correctness

5.8 Override Protocols & Emergency Stop

Override is required when: CV reflects non-standard experience NovaHire may not appreciate; industry/role context is outside training data; bias indicators in score patterns; gut-feel mismatch between score and candidate quality; any concern about fairness. Each override is logged with timestamp, reviewer ID, candidate ID, and reason code.

Emergency stop: any authorized user can pause NovaHire for their organization instantly; NovaAI Labs can remote-suspend NovaHire for a specific deployer if a critical issue is detected; the Chief AI Officer has authority to globally suspend NovaHire.

5.9 Effectiveness Monitoring & Escalation

Tracked metrics: override rate per deployer; distribution of override reasons; time spent per review; reviewer-identity diversity (no single person approving 100% of shortlists); correlation of override patterns with demographic outcomes; customer feedback on oversight burden.

Escalation chain: HR coordinator → HR lead → HR Director → AI Governance Office → AI Governance Committee → Chief AI Officer → Board AI Safety Sub-Committee (for critical issues).

6. Document 5 — Logging & Traceability

Doc ID: NVA-HRAI-DOC-05 • Classification: EU AI Act Article 12 — Record-keeping.

6.1 Logging Philosophy — Five Principles

  1. Logs as Evidence — sufficient to fully reconstruct any hiring decision; a regulator or plaintiff should be able to ask "what exactly happened to this candidate?" and receive a complete answer.
  2. Logs as Accountability — human actions logged alongside AI actions, making clear who was responsible at each stage.
  3. Logs as Learning — enable post-hoc analysis of edge cases, failures, and fairness concerns; used by the AI Governance Office to identify patterns and retrain models.
  4. Logs as Protection — protect deployers (documenting their use), reviewers (showing diligence), and candidates (creating a record for grievances).
  5. Privacy-Respecting — PII is minimized: candidate IDs hashed, sensitive data anonymized where possible, retention limited to minimum required.

6.2 Six Log Categories

A. System OperationAPI calls and responses, model inference operations, authN/authZ events, configuration changes, version/model identifier, errors and exceptions

6.4 Access Controls & Integrity

Role-based access: deployers can access their own customer-specific logs; the AI Governance Office has enterprise oversight access; the DPO accesses logs for privacy inquiries; regulators receive access on request per Article 12(5); the audit team gets time-limited, purpose-specific access. Separation of duties: those who approve decisions cannot audit their own logs; meta-logging of all log access; access requests logged with purpose and time range.

Integrity controls: cryptographic hash chain for decision logs (tamper detection); WORM (write-once-read-many) storage for audit logs; immutable append-only structure (no deletion, only expiration); regular integrity verification via hash validation; incident response protocol for any integrity breach.

6.5 Log Format & Example

All logs are structured JSON (not free-text), OpenTelemetry-compatible for distributed tracing, ISO 8601 UTC timestamps, with a standard schema published for deployer ingestion. Correlation IDs link API calls to decisions across systems.

{
  "log_id": "dlog-2026-04-10-abc123",
  "timestamp": "2026-04-10T14:32:15Z",
  "candidate_id_hash": "sha256:a7f8e2c1...",
  "model_version": "nova-v2.3.1",
  "confidence_score": 0.87,
  "feature_importance": {
    "skills_match": 0.42,
    "experience_level": 0.38,
    "education": 0.20
  },
  "decision": "shortlist",
  "correlation_id": "req-xyz789"
}

6.6 Personal Data, Monitoring & Export

  • Candidate IDs are SHA-256 hashed, never raw names or email addresses
  • Raw CV/application contents are never logged
  • Reviewer identities logged for accountability purposes
  • DSAR: hashed IDs linked back via secure lookup; deletion requests unlink the candidate pseudonym while preserving the audit trail

Real-time alerts: unusual review patterns (e.g., rapid approvals without reading scores), fairness metric breaches, authentication anomalies (off-hours access), and error spikes. Daily dashboards for the AI Governance Office, weekly summaries for deployers (their data only), and monthly compliance reports.

Regulator export: standard JSON/CSV format on request; 5 business day turnaround for routine, 24-hour turnaround for urgent regulatory requests; deployer-specific extracts and time-range queries supported; secure, encrypted transmission.

6.7 Governance & Limitations

Joint ownership: CISO + AI Governance Office. Quarterly policy review, AI Governance Committee approval for changes, annual audit of log completeness by Internal Audit.

Honest limitations: logs capture what happened, not why a human decided as they did beyond documented reasoning; aggregated statistics may not detect subtle individual-level discrimination; logging is not preventive — it enables after-the-fact analysis only; no log survives deletion requests beyond pseudonymized archives.

7. Document 6 — Performance Monitoring & Post-Market Surveillance

Doc ID: NVA-HRAI-DOC-06 • Classification: EU AI Act Articles 15 (accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity) + 61 (post-market monitoring).

7.1 Monitoring Philosophy

  • Monitoring must cover accuracy and fairness from day one
  • Both technical metrics and real-world outcomes matter
  • Early detection of drift and degradation is essential
  • Monitoring must be continuous, not periodic snapshots
  • Post-market surveillance is a provider obligation, not deployer burden

7.2 Six Performance Metric Categories

7.2.1 Accuracy

Top-k accuracy • Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR) • Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain (NDCG) • Precision/Recall at k • F1 for binary shortlist decisions • Calibration (does fit score 80 correspond to 80% match likelihood?).

7.2.2 Fairness

Demographic parity • equalized odds • equal opportunity • disparate impact ratio (4/5 rule) • counterfactual fairness • individual fairness • intersectional fairness (gender × age, race × gender, etc.).

7.2.3 Robustness

Adversarial accuracy under CV manipulation • out-of-distribution detection rate • performance on edge cases (non-standard CVs, multilingual, career changers) • stability under input perturbation • confidence calibration under uncertainty.

7.2.4 Cybersecurity

Prompt injection success rate (NLP input) • API abuse detection rate • authentication bypass attempts • data exfiltration attempts detected.

7.2.5 Operational

Latency (p50/p95/p99) • throughput (req/s) • availability (uptime %) • error rate • model inference success rate.

7.2.6 Real-World Outcomes

Candidate interview-to-shortlist rate (did humans agree with NovaHire?) • performance and retention of hired candidates from NovaHire shortlists • candidate complaint rates • override rates (from Doc 4) • deployer satisfaction scores.

7.4 Monitoring Infrastructure & Data Drift

Infrastructure: real-time metric computation pipeline; four dashboard tiers (Technical for ML engineering, Governance for AGO, Executive for CAIO, Deployer for enterprise customers); alert routing to on-call engineers and the AI Governance Office; historical metric retention for trend analysis.

Drift monitoring: input distribution monitoring (CV characteristics drift over time); prediction distribution monitoring; concept drift (relationship between CV features and job fit changes); population drift (applicant pool demographic shifts); automated drift detection with statistical tests; drift thresholds trigger review.

7.7 Article 73 Incident Reporting

Serious incidents reported to the national competent authority within 15 days; material issues escalated per the incident response procedure; deployer-reported issues triaged within 24 hours; candidate complaints monitored as an early signal.

7.8 Improvement, Robustness & Cybersecurity

Performance improvement process: quarterly model evaluation cycle; improvement proposals reviewed by the AI Governance Committee before deployment; A/B testing framework for controlled rollout; gradual rollout with monitoring; rollback capability if degradation is detected.

Robustness testing: continuous red team exercises; adversarial example generation; edge-case library maintained and tested against each release; stress testing of infrastructure; chaos engineering for resilience.

Cybersecurity (Article 15): application security monitoring, network monitoring, authN/authZ monitoring, data loss prevention, model extraction attempt detection, API abuse detection and rate limiting, bi-annual penetration testing, and a bug bounty programme.

7.9 Deployer Reporting & Model Update Management

Deployers receive: monthly performance report for their deployment; quarterly fairness assessment; annual compliance attestation; incident notifications as they occur; model update notifications with impact assessment.

Model updates: all material updates go through the AI Use Case Review process; impact assessment before deployment; rollback plan documented; deployer notification minimum 14 days before material updates; post-update monitoring intensified for 30 days.

7.10 Transparency Reporting

Quarterly transparency report including NovaHire performance and fairness metrics, published on trust.novaai.example. Aggregated metrics protect individual privacy; methodology disclosed.
