Cyber & AI Risk Program — TradeFlow Inc.

End-to-end cyber and AI risk program for a multi-regulated fintech broker-dealer — 12-risk cyber register (NIST CSF & ISO 27001), 10-risk AI register for TradeFlow Insight (NIST AI RMF & EU AI Act), third-party vendor risk assessment, 3-jurisdiction regulatory mapping (US / EU / UK), and an AI governance policy with kill switch and monitoring thresholds

Company: TradeFlow Inc. (~250 employees)
Industry: Fintech / Broker-Dealer API
Frameworks: NIST CSF, ISO 27001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, GDPR, MiFID II, DORA
Scale: 200 institutional clients • 6M retail accounts • 35 countries

Project Overview

TradeFlow Inc. is a US-headquartered, self-clearing broker-dealer providing brokerage infrastructure via institutional-grade APIs. The platform powers approximately 6 million retail brokerage accounts through 200 institutional clients (fintechs, advisors, wealth managers, hedge funds) across 35 countries. TradeFlow is SEC-registered, FINRA-authorised in the US, MiFID II-authorised via Dublin, and FCA-authorised in London. In January 2026, TradeFlow launched TradeFlow Insight — a transformer-based AI recommendation engine delivering personalised stock and ETF suggestions to ~1.8 million accounts across 62 adopting clients.

This project delivers a complete cyber and AI risk program covering the full TradeFlow platform: a 12-risk cyber register aligned to NIST CSF and ISO/IEC 27001, a 10-risk AI-specific register aligned to NIST AI RMF and the EU AI Act, a third-party vendor risk assessment of the critical market data provider MarketBridge Data Solutions, a full regulatory landscape mapping across US (SEC, FINRA), EU (EU AI Act, GDPR, MiFID II, DORA), and UK (FCA), and a comprehensive AI Governance Policy defining the governance committee, model lifecycle, kill switch procedures, monitoring thresholds, and AI incident response.

Project Highlights

Platform ScopeBrokerage API, Market Data Service, and TradeFlow Insight (AI recommendation engine) — deployed on GCP across us-central1 and europe-west1 regions
Cyber Risks Assessed12 cyber risks on a 5×5 Likelihood–Impact matrix — from API unauthorised access and cloud misconfiguration to ransomware and BC/DR failure — mapped to NIST CSF functions and ISO 27001 Annex A controls. All residual risks reduced to Medium or Low.
AI Risks Assessed10 AI-specific risks for TradeFlow Insight — bias, explainability, regulatory classification, data governance, model drift, adversarial manipulation, hallucination, privacy leakage, prompt injection, and lack of human oversight — aligned to NIST AI RMF (Govern / Map / Measure / Manage) and EU AI Act Articles 6, 10, 13, 14, 15
Vendor Risk AssessmentFull 8-area security assessment of MarketBridge Data Solutions covering information security programme, access controls, encryption, incident response, BC/DR, data handling, subprocessor management, and regulatory compliance — plus 6-risk vendor register and contractual protections (SLA, audit rights, 24-hour breach notification)
Regulatory Mapping3-jurisdiction mapping: US (SEC registration, FINRA rules 4530/6730, Reg SCI, 17a-4 recordkeeping, Rule 15c3-3), EU (EU AI Act Reg. 2024/1689, GDPR Articles 22/33/34, MiFID II, DORA operational resilience), UK (FCA, UK GDPR, Consumer Duty)
AI Governance PolicyGovernance structure (AI Governance Committee, Model Owner, Risk Owner), model lifecycle gates (Development, Pre-Deployment Review, Production Monitoring, Change Management, Retirement), 15-minute global kill switch, client-level disable, and critical threshold monitoring (accuracy, diversity, fairness gap, drift, latency)
Critical Regulatory TensionIdentified the single highest-severity issue: classification of TradeFlow Insight as “investment advice” vs “information” under SEC Rule 206(4)-1 and FINRA Rule 4512 — scored Critical (25/25) inherent — with legal-engagement mitigation plan reducing residual to Medium
