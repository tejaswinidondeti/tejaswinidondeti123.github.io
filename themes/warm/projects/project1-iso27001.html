ISO 27001:2022 Risk Assessment — SaaS Cloud Migration

End-to-end risk assessment for a B2B SaaS company migrating 5M+ customer records to AWS

Company: Tech Start Inc (500 employees)
Industry: B2B SaaS / Cloud
Frameworks: ISO 27001:2022, NIST CSF

1. Executive Summary

Tech Start Inc is a B2B SaaS company serving enterprise clients with a customer database containing over 5 million records. The company is migrating this critical database from on-premises infrastructure to Amazon Web Services (AWS) to improve scalability, reduce operational costs, and meet growing client demands. Simultaneously, Tech Start is pursuing ISO 27001:2022 certification to satisfy enterprise client security requirements and demonstrate a mature information security posture.

This risk assessment identifies critical assets involved in the migration, evaluates key risks using a 5x5 likelihood-impact matrix, and maps security controls to ISO 27001:2022 Annex A and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework. The goal is to ensure that the migration is executed securely, with risks reduced to acceptable levels, while building the foundation for ISO 27001 certification.

2. Risk Scoring Methodology

We use a 5x5 matrix where Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact. Scores are categorized as: Low (1-4), Medium (5-9), High (10-14), Critical (15-25).

Likelihood Scale

1 — RareLess than once in 5 years (e.g., zero-day targeting your specific stack)
2 — UnlikelyOnce every 2-5 years (e.g., disgruntled employee data theft attempt)
3 — PossibleOnce per year (e.g., successful phishing email)
4 — LikelyMultiple times per year (e.g., cloud misconfiguration from rapid deployment)
5 — Almost CertainMonthly or more (e.g., employees reusing weak passwords)

Impact Scale

1 — Negligible< $10K loss. No customer impact.
2 — Minor$10K-$100K loss. Brief service disruption.
3 — Moderate$100K-$500K loss. Service degraded for hours. Media coverage possible.
4 — Major$500K-$2M loss. Extended outage. Customer churn. Regulatory inquiry.
5 — Severe> $2M loss. Massive data breach. Lawsuits. Loss of ISO certification.

4. Risk Identification & Assessment

R-01: Unauthorized Access to Customer Database During Migration

CRITICAL — Score: 20 (L:4 x I:5)

When migrating the database to AWS, sensitive customer data (PII, payment details) could be exposed through unencrypted network connections, excessive employee access, unremoved temporary credentials, or unencrypted backups. A breach could result in privacy law penalties, financial fines, customer loss, and reputation damage.

R-02: AWS Account Compromise via Misconfigured IAM

CRITICAL — Score: 20 (L:4 x I:5)

AWS permissions might be set incorrectly, granting engineers or service accounts more access than needed. During migrations, teams often grant broad permissions to resolve access issues and forget to revoke them, leading to potential accidental system deletion, data theft, unsafe root account usage, or exposed access keys in code.

R-03: Data Loss or Corruption During Migration

CRITICAL — Score: 15 (L:3 x I:5)

During transfer of ~2TB of customer data, records could be lost, duplicated, damaged, or stored incorrectly due to format differences, network interruptions, replication delays, schema mismatches, or large field handling issues.

R-04: Data Residency Non-Compliance

HIGH — Score: 12 (L:3 x I:4)

Post-migration, data might be stored or processed in cloud regions not permitted by privacy laws or customer contracts. Non-compliance could result in fines, customer loss, or certification failure.

R-05: Extended Service Outage During Cutover

HIGH — Score: 12 (L:3 x I:4)

During the database cutover, technical problems (connection failures, network misconfiguration, failed rollback, extended migration time) could cause prolonged downtime, breaching SLAs and causing customer impact.

6. Security Controls

C-01: End-to-End Data Encryption (AES-256 + TLS 1.3)

Mitigates: R-01  |  ISO 27001: Annex A 8.24  |  NIST CSF: PR.DS-1, PR.DS-2

All customer data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Only secure connections allowed. Encryption keys rotated regularly with all key activities logged.

C-02: Least-Privilege IAM with MFA Enforcement

Mitigates: R-02  |  ISO 27001: Annex A 5.15, 5.16, 8.5  |  NIST CSF: PR.AC-1, PR.AC-4

Role-based access with minimum permissions. Root account secured with hardware MFA (YubiKey). Temporary credentials replace permanent access keys. Quarterly access reviews.

C-03: Automated Backup & Data Validation

Mitigates: R-03  |  ISO 27001: Annex A 8.13, 8.14  |  NIST CSF: PR.IP-4

Full pre-migration backup stored in two locations. Automated validation compares source/target databases (row counts, checksums, foreign keys). Original database kept read-only for 30 days post-migration.

C-04: AWS Region Restriction with SCP Enforcement

Mitigates: R-04  |  ISO 27001: Annex A 5.34, 5.31  |  NIST CSF: ID.GV-3

Service Control Policies block resource creation in unapproved regions. AWS Config rules continuously monitor compliance. Automated alerts and tagging for non-compliant resources.

C-05: Cutover Runbook with Tested Rollback

Mitigates: R-05  |  ISO 27001: Annex A 5.30, 8.14  |  NIST CSF: PR.IP-9, RC.RP-1

Detailed step-by-step cutover guide with decision trees. Two rehearsals in staging (target: 45-min cutover, 15-min rollback). Automated rollback triggers if error rate >5% or latency >2s.

8. Risk Treatment Plans

A Risk Treatment Plan (RTP) is the action plan that takes a risk from ‘identified’ to ‘managed’. While the risk register tells you WHAT the risks are and the controls tell you HOW to reduce them, the treatment plan tells you WHO will do WHAT, by WHEN, with WHAT budget, and HOW you will know it worked.

Risk Treatment Plan 1: Unauthorized Access to Customer Database (R-01)

Risk IDR-01
Inherent Score20 (Critical) — L:4 × I:5
TreatmentMITIGATE
Target Score8 (Medium) — L:2 × I:4
Owner / ApproverVP of Engineering / CISO

Treatment Actions

  • Enable AES-256 encryption at rest using AWS KMS with automatic key rotation
  • Enforce TLS 1.3 for all database connections; disable unencrypted access
  • Restrict database access to approved app servers within private VPC only (no public endpoint)
  • Route migration traffic through private AWS Direct Connect
  • Deploy DLP tool to detect accidental PII exposure in cloud storage and logs
  • Conduct penetration test on database and migration pipeline before go-live
  • Enable database activity monitoring with real-time alerts for anomalies (large downloads, unknown IPs, off-hours access)
  • Remove all temporary migration credentials within 24 hours of completion

Risk Treatment Plan 2: AWS IAM Misconfiguration (R-02)

Risk IDR-02
Inherent Score20 (Critical) — L:4 × I:5
TreatmentMITIGATE
Target Score5 (Medium) — L:1 × I:5
Owner / ApproverDirector of Cloud Infrastructure / CISO

Treatment Actions

  • Lock root account with hardware MFA (YubiKey); remove root access keys; store credentials in physical safe
  • Create RBAC policies for 4 groups: Developers, DBAs, DevOps, Security — each scoped to exact resources
  • Enforce MFA for all IAM users; block access if MFA is not enabled
  • Replace all long-term access keys with temporary credentials via IAM roles and STS AssumeRole
  • Run weekly reports to detect any newly created permanent access keys
  • Deploy IAM Access Analyzer to detect overly broad permissions and external access
  • Conduct quarterly access reviews with team leaders; remove unused roles/permissions

Risk Treatment Plan 3: Data Loss/Corruption During Migration (R-03)

Risk IDR-03
Inherent Score15 (Critical) — L:3 × I:5
TreatmentMITIGATE
Target Score6 (Medium) — L:2 × I:3
Owner / ApproverLead Database Engineer / VP of Engineering

Treatment Actions

  • Create full pre-migration backup stored in two locations (local NAS + encrypted S3 bucket)
  • Verify backup by restoring to test server and comparing record counts
  • Build automated validation scripts (Python/SQL) — row counts, numeric checksums, MD5 hashes in 10K batches, foreign key checks, character encoding validation
  • Run 3 complete migration rehearsals in staging; require 100% validation pass rate
  • Configure AWS DMS with validation enabled; set CloudWatch alerts for replication lag > 60 seconds
  • Keep on-prem database in read-only mode for 30 days post-migration as rollback safety net
  • Auto-rollback if data validation errors exceed 0.01% of total records

Risk Treatment Plan 4: Data Residency Compliance Violation (R-04)

Risk IDR-04
Inherent Score12 (High) — L:3 × I:4
TreatmentMITIGATE
Target Score4 (Low) — L:1 × I:4
Owner / ApproverData Protection Officer / Chief Legal Officer

Treatment Actions

  • Conduct DPIA (GDPR Article 35) — map all PII data flows: origin, processing, storage, and access
  • Create Data Residency Matrix linking customer segments to required AWS regions (e.g., EU clients → eu-west-1); get Legal sign-off
  • Implement SCPs to block resource creation in unapproved regions (even for admins)
  • Deploy AWS Config rules for continuous region compliance monitoring
  • Set up automated alerts via SNS + Lambda tagging for non-compliant resources
  • Update all client DPAs to specify approved AWS regions; include 48-hour change notification clause

Risk Treatment Plan 5: Extended Cutover Outage (R-05)

Risk IDR-05
Inherent Score12 (High) — L:3 × I:4
TreatmentMITIGATE
Target Score4 (Low) — L:2 × I:2
Owner / ApproverVP of Engineering / CTO

Treatment Actions

  • Create detailed cutover runbook with step-by-step commands, owners, timings, success criteria, and decision trees
  • Have 3 engineers independently review the runbook
  • Rehearsal 1: Full staging cutover with production data copy — target 45 min; document and fix issues
  • Rehearsal 2: Staged rollback test — verify full restore with zero data loss in under 15 min
  • Set up real-time dashboard (CloudWatch + Grafana) — error rates, connections, latency, DMS lag
  • Configure auto-rollback triggers: error rate > 5% for 5 min or latency > 2s for 10 min
  • Customer communication plan: 7-day notice, 24-hour reminder, live status page, post-cutover confirmation
  • Assign Customer Success lead for real-time escalations during cutover window
