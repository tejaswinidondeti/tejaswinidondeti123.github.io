ISO 27001 Risk Assessment

ISO 27001:2022 Cloud Migration Programme — TechStart Inc.

End-to-end ISMS implementation and cloud migration risk programme — 7-risk register, 24-task remediation tracker, 93-control Statement of Applicability, and 3 core ISMS policies

Company: TechStart Inc. (500 employees)
Industry: B2B SaaS / Cloud Services
Frameworks: ISO/IEC 27001:2022, ISO/IEC 27002:2022, NIST CSF, GDPR
Scope: 5M+ customer records, 2 TB AWS migration

Project Overview

TechStart Inc. is a 500-employee B2B SaaS company migrating its primary customer database — over 5 million records totaling 2 TB — from on-premises infrastructure to AWS Cloud. In parallel, TechStart is pursuing ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification to satisfy enterprise client security requirements and demonstrate a mature information security posture.

This programme delivers the full Information Security Management System (ISMS) foundation aligned to ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and mapped to the NIST Cybersecurity Framework: a formal risk assessment of the migration, 3 core ISMS policies, a live risk register and 5×5 heat map, a remediation tracker driving treatment to closure, and a complete Statement of Applicability across all 93 Annex A controls. The programme explicitly targets the highest-impact migration risks — unauthorised database access, IAM misconfiguration, data corruption during cutover, data residency under GDPR, and extended service outage — and drives every residual risk down to Medium or Low.

Project Highlights

Critical Assets IdentifiedCustomer PII Database (5M+ records, RDS), AWS Production Account (IAM, KMS, CloudTrail), and the AWS DMS Migration Pipeline (2 TB transfer)
Risks Assessed7 risks on a 5×5 Likelihood–Impact matrix — 2 Critical, 1 High, 3 Medium-High, 1 Medium inherent — all reduced to Medium or Low residual after treatment
Security Controls Designed5 detailed controls covering encryption (AES-256 + TLS 1.2+), least-privilege IAM with MFA, automated backup & data validation, region-lock SCPs for data residency, and a rehearsed cutover runbook with rollback plan
ISMS Policies Authored3 core policies: Information Security Policy, Access Control Policy, and Data Classification & Handling Policy — each aligned to ISO/IEC 27002:2022 Annex A references
Statement of ApplicabilityAll 93 Annex A controls assessed: 92 Applicable, 60 Implemented, 27 Partially Implemented, rest Planned — with documented justification, evidence, and linked risks for each
Remediation Tracker24 remediation tasks tracked with owners, priorities, target dates, and evidence links — weekly cadence during migration, biweekly post-cutover, P1 items escalated to CISO on overdue
Frameworks AppliedISO/IEC 27001:2022 (Annex A), ISO/IEC 27002:2022, ISO 31000:2018 risk methodology, NIST CSF (Identify/Protect/Detect/Respond/Recover), GDPR Art. 33–35, CIS AWS Foundations
Regulatory ScopeEU GDPR (data residency, breach notification, DPIA), CCPA, enterprise customer DPA obligations, and AWS shared-responsibility model compliance
